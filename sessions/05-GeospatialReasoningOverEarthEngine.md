# Session 5 - Ask the Planet: Geospatial Reasoning over Earth Engine for Kenya's Climate Risk

- **Speakers**: [John M. Megwe](https://www.linkedin.com/in/john-mwangi-megwe-539982222/)

## About the session
At I/O 2026 Google unveiled the Gemini-powered Geospatial Reasoning agent, it turns a plain-English question into a multi-step plan, calls expert sub-agents across Earth Engine and Data Commons, and fuses the results into an explainable answer. The catch: it's gated behind Trusted Tester access, so most of us can't touch it.

So we rebuilt the pattern in the open and run it live on Kenyan data. Using Gemini 3.5 Flash as the planner in front of Earth Engine tools, we ask one real question, along the Tana River, which settlements face the highest flood exposure, and how many people live there? You'll watch the agent decompose the question, pull a Sentinel-derived flood signal and elevation from Earth Engine, intersect it with population data, and rank the most vulnerable settlements on a map.

You'll leave with a forkable repo and a Colab notebook, point the same agent at drought, deforestation, or any African geospatial question in under an hour.
